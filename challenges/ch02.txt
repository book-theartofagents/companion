Challenge 2: Burn the wallet on purpose
========================================
Paired with Chapter 2 (Waging War) and chapters/02-waging-war/

Context
-------
The gateway in chapters/02-waging-war/ tracks tokens per call and refuses
work that exceeds budget. The golden dataset has a mix of cheap lookups
and expensive summarisations. The anti-pattern demo shows what happens
when every request runs through the biggest model available.

Challenge
---------
1. Run `python chapters/02-waging-war/run-eval.py` and note the total
   cost reported.
2. Run `python chapters/02-waging-war/anti-pattern-demo.py` and note its
   cost.
3. The gap between the two is your thesis. Write it down as one sentence
   in a comment at the top of your notes file.
4. Now break the discipline on purpose. Pick one query from the dataset
   and route it through the expensive model even though the router said
   otherwise. Measure the cost delta.
5. Restore the routing. Prove with a diff that you are back to baseline.

Constraints
-----------
- You must log the cost of every call. No guesses.
- Do not add a new model. Use what the gateway already knows about.

Success criterion
-----------------
A short table in your notes showing: baseline cost, anti-pattern cost,
your deliberate miss, restored cost. The table should make it obvious
why the gateway exists.

Where to compare
----------------
If your deliberate miss cost less than the anti-pattern total, the
gateway is already doing most of the work. If it cost more, you found
a real hole in the routing rules. Either outcome teaches something.
