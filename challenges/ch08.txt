Challenge 8: Pick the wrong formation on purpose
=================================================
Paired with Chapter 8 (Variation in Tactics) and chapters/08-variation-in-tactics/

Context
-------
Chapter 8 maps four formations: single-agent, pipeline, router, and
swarm. Each fits a different shape of problem. The example in
chapters/08-variation-in-tactics/ runs the same task through all four
and shows the cost and accuracy trade-offs.

Challenge
---------
1. Run `python chapters/08-variation-in-tactics/run-eval.py`. Read the
   four formation outputs and the summary table.
2. For each formation, write one sentence describing the shape of
   problem it fits best.
3. Invert the recommendation: take a scenario where the router is
   correct and force it through the swarm. Measure the cost. Observe
   what the swarm does with work that did not need four agents.
4. Now take a scenario where the swarm was recommended and force it
   through the single agent. Watch it degrade or fail.
5. In your notes, state the threshold at which swarm stops being
   overkill. Pick one metric: tokens, accuracy, wall time.

Constraints
-----------
- Use the exact formations shipped in the chapter. Do not invent a
  fifth.
- Your threshold must be a number a colleague could apply without
  re-reading the chapter.

Success criterion
-----------------
A one-page decision table: formation, best fit in one sentence, cost
at baseline, what breaks when you force it. Bring it to your next
planning meeting.

Where to compare
----------------
If your notes say "it depends", you have not picked a threshold. The
point of the formations is that defaults are cheap and defaults are
wrong often. Commit to a number.
